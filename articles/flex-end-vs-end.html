end vs. flex-end in flexboxes

September 21, 2025

The key difference between flex-end and end is context. The former is tied to the flex container's direction, while the latter is a logical value tied to the writing direction of the language (e.g., Left-to-Right vs. Right-to-Left).

This distinction is crucial for creating layouts that adapt correctly to international audiences. When flex-direction is set to row-reverse, for instance, flex-end will push items to the left. However, end will still push items to the right in English (a LTR language), because that is the logical "end" of the line.
